PLAN IN DREAM

 

SUBRAHMANYA BHARATI

(Rendered into English from the original Tamil by K. C. Kamaliah)

 

We’ll take a walk, my dear, on the mountain of silvery snow;

We’ll let our ships cruise in the entire western sea;

We’ll make temples of all our centres of learning;

Cry we: ‘This is our Bharat Country:’ with passion burning.

 

We’ll construct a bridge to the Sinhala Island;

We’ll build up the Setu and construct an avenue

With the surplus waters gushing over Bengal,

We’ll pursue farming in the central region.

 

Digging many mines and from gold onward,

We’ll drill and bring more things forward.

Reaching the eight quarters and marketing these,

We’ll bring home to the country wealth profuse.

 

It’s in the southern sea they dive for pearls.

Merchants of many lauds storming the market,

Bring us useful commodities with a clamour

And crave in the west coast for our goodwill as their armour.

 

In the waters of the Indus on moonlight night,

With the youthful maidens of flawless Chera Country,

Humming songs in elegant Telugu,

We’ll revel in play by rowing boats.

 

Wheat and wheat product of the Gangetic delta

We’ll exchange for the Cauvery betel leaf.

For the verse of the leonine Maratta,

We’ll present them with Chera ivory.

 

The discourse delivered by the poets of Kashi,

We’ll make an instrument to hear at Kanchi.

To them, the warriors Rajputana,

We’ll present pure gold from Kannada.

 

Making cloth of cotton and apparel of silk,

We’ll throw heaps of them as hillock.

To the merchants of far-off lands bringing treasure,

We’ll give them in plenty with pleasure.

 

Tools we’ll make and paper we’ll make;

Establish factories we’ll and institutions of learning.

Tarry not we’ll, nor lean our heads for a nap.

Truths we’ll tell and many charities we’ll practise.

 

Umbrellas we’ll make and make we’ll ploughing implements.

Gunnies we’ll make and make we’ll nails of iron.

Carriages we’ll produce, swift and fleeting.

Build we’ll ships of war as to shake the world.

 

Learn we’ll the mantras and expertise of professions.

Span we’ll the heavens and the sea we’ll fathom.

How the lunar system functions, with clarity we’ll find.

Learn we’ll sciences at street corners and road avenues.

 

Epics we’ll write and thick forests we’ll grow.

Arts we’ll patronise and strengthen we’ll blacksmithy.

Paintings we’ll draw and make we’ll good spindles.

With pleasure we’ll take up the jobs in the world.

 

“There is no other caste after two,” said the Tamil woman.*

That only is ambrosia true.

Straight men of ethical code helpful to society are superior;

The others are inferior.

 

* Poetess Auvaiyar
