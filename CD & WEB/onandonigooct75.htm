ON AND ON I GO

 

The horizon is still far,

But light is dimming;

I’ve been coming along,

Over ups and downs,

Through and across.

Why, where, what for?

In fatigue I sit for a while,

But no rest they give– 

The aching body, restless mind;

Lures of hope and dreams;

Fancies and fantacies;

The deceiving eye;

The call of alluring Future,

Thrust of the dead Past,

And exciting stir of the Present.

I rise and move on again and on;

Why, where, what for?

I ask, wonder, ask again,

But horizon holds the view.

O, where, why, how far?

In despair I stare at the night star.

–JATINDRA MOHAN GANGULI
