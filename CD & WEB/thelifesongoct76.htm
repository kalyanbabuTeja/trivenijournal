THE LIFE-SONG

 

NARD MORANTI

 

The organs

what to do with

to let them go to the origin

another option

another wish

of the absent-minded god

never fulfilled

 

one eats the unedible

the second to speak lie alone

the third’s business of

hearing a boredom

for its helplessness

the smelling organ smells all foul

 

Look, look at

do not look into things

the fifth getting squeezed thus

and the control-room’s

closure an historic event

 

the day the heaven and hell

became one

it observes a routine

to pass salty water out

the other that precipitate all my nerves

to action

ends daily on a plain wood-cot

the dark night as a trustworthy companion

my sorrows my heaven.
