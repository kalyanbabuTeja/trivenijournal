Love's Home-Coming

BY PATTUMARATTU-MOSI-KEERANAR

(Translated by P. N. Appuswami from the original poem in Tamil)

[A travelling minstrel tells a maid that her lover is coming home. She is overjoyed and is unable to believe her ears. And she eagerly questions the minstrel about it.]

Did you see with your own eyes?

Or heard you, perhaps, from one who saw?

The truth I would hear; oh tell it me.

So shall you have golden Patali

By the Sone, where white-tusked elephants bathe,

Tell me: from whose lips heard you

Of my lover's coming?
