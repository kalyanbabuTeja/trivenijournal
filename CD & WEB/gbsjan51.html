G. B. S.

 

O immortality, your children, too,

alas, are mortal; even so is their

mortality a child of yours!...O you

master playwright, you conquered the despair

of life; so might you conquer the malaise

of death. But oh, to think that you no longer

teach us, no longer write, on longer raise

your voice of wisdom–wisdom that was stronger

than all the world’s philosophies–this breaks

our hearts. And yet, your deathless spirit makes

us trust that there where you lie buried, or

there where you dwell in starry heavens, you may

still reign o’er human thought and shine like star

and sun and moon in an eternal way!

 

–RADHAKRISHNA MOORTHY CHALLA

(From his book Passion & Philosophy)
