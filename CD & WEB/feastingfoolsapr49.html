FEASTING FOOLS

 

By Mukunda

 

THE nodding trees

greet the nomad winds,

when fruits fall

and flowers wither;

when words fall

and thoughts wither,

the feasting fools

greet each other.

 

The roaring seas

greet the scorching sands,

when steamers scream

and robbers loot;

when poets weep

and harlots croon,

the feasting fools

greet each other.

 

The lightning grins

greet the twinkling orbs,

when mothers bleed

and infants kick;

when gallows bleed

and gods they die,

the feasting fools

greet each other.
