HIMALAYAS: IDEOLOGICAL FRONTIER BETWEEN

DEMOCRACY AND COMMUNISM

 

H. V. KAMATH

 

For the first time in history, India has been militarily attacked by China. Never before had the high Himalayas been pierced by an enemy army’s trucks and tanks, nor the deep silence rudely disturbed by murderous mortars and shells. The shock to India has been all the greater, because the bloody assault has been launched by a neighbour for whom India had done more than any other country during the past decade and whose Premier Chou En-Lai had often been affectionately embraced by our Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru in the true spirit of Panchasheel, to the resonant refrain of ‘Hindi-Chini Bhai-Bhai.’

 

Our trust has been betrayed by Communist China; the hand of friendship held out by India has been spurned. Nehru has confessed that for years he had lived “in a dream world, out of touch with reality,” and President Radhakrishnan sadly observed that Government had been too credulous and negligent in the past. But it need not have been so, had they heeded the warnings sounded by friends from time to time since the military subjugation of Tibet by China in 1950. When Nehru protested, though mildly in Parliament against China’s invasion of Tibet he was branded as ‘the running dog of British imperialism’ by Communist China’s Government and the Press. He subsequently succumbed to the arts and wiles of Chinese Chou En-Lai, and acquiesced in China’s military conquest of Tibet. India then sowed the wind, and now she reaps the whirlwind.

 

By that ruthless action, carried out in the worst colonial, imperialist style, China sounded the tocsin for India and South-East Asia. Pandit Nehru and his Government were, however, blind to the Red Signal and to the menacing advance of the Communist Chinese hordes.

 

Chinese Idea of Himalayan Federation: In 1959, after a two months’ tour of the India-Tibet border, I wrote in July of that year that “Nehru will have to adopt a firmer attitude towards China, and her colonialism in Tibet must be consistently exposed and condemned, just as he has castigated European imperialism in the past. His supine policy will, if unaltered, bring disaster to the Indian people. India’s foreign policy should be reoriented towards freedom, democracy, spiritual values and God.” Further I stated that “China is toying with the idea of a Himalayan Federation consisting of Ladakh, Nepal, Sikkim, Bhutan and Nefa under China’s hegemony or suzerainty, and her foreign policy in the next few years will seek to drive a wedge between India on the one hand and Nepal, Sikkim and Bhutan on the other. If India’s rulers continue to drift without vigilance and without vision, the Himalayan kingdoms of Nepal, Sikkim and Bhutan will gradually be sucked into the orbit of Communist China.” While touring the region I noticed “the insidious propaganda carried on by China’s agents on the Himalayan border for the inclusion of all Mongolian and Mongoloid people in the ‘great Chinese family’ and asked: “With the Himalayan buffer, Tibet, gone, how can India, in the face of all these developments feel safe and secure?” I was convinced that “the communization of south and South-East Asia under China’s hegemony or leadership is the programme set by her Communist leaders for the first 25 years of their regime,” and asserted that “If our Government pursues a firm and vigilant foreign and defence policy serving India’s interests first, all may yet be well with India, and China’s plan will come to naught. Else, menaced by China in the North, and assailed by Pakistan in the East and the West, India’s freedom will not endure.” I had no doubt that “we must force the Chinese army to quit India now, otherwise it may become almost impossible later on,” and clearly hinted that “there is a vague notion that the relations between Russia and China are not as cordial as they were a couple of years ago, but it would be unwise to rely on this so-called rift. Despite appearances to the contrary, Russia and China will stand side by side.”

 

Tragic Truth: What I had foreseen more than three years ago has now tragically come true. Persistent neglect of defence preparation and a policy of appeasement based on the vain hope that Chini bhai would thereby be dissuaded from attacking his, Hindi bhai, only served to whet the appetite of the aggressor, just as the then British Prime Minister Chamberlain’s policy of appeasement of Hitler in the thirties culminated in the Second World war. Here the undeclared war by China has, after taking a heavy toll of our men and material, terminated, for the time being in an undemanded ceasefire.

 

The Chinese proposals are, briefly, that both sides withdraw to the line of actual Control as on November 7, 1959 along the entire border; that both Withdraw 20 kilometres (12 ½ miles) from this line, and the two Prime Ministers should meet thereafter to negotiate the ‘border dispute.’ Our Prime Minister has rightly rejected these proposals, and suggested instead that the Chinese withdraw to the line of September 8, 1962 before talks could start. If the Chinese line were accepted, India would not be allowed to reoccupy territories under its control on September 8, 1962 and will thus have to yield;

 

in Nefa: Dhola, Kinzemane, Walong and Kibitoo

in Ladakh: 43 check posts we built between 1959 and 1962.

 

This would be in addition to the demand that we withdraw 20 K.M. (12½ miles) from our own territory in all the three sectors including the middle sector of Uttar Pradesh. It is no Wonder that China’s proposals, which are but a treacherous manoeuvre, have evoked universal condemnation in India, barring the Communist party.

 

Role of the C. P. I.: The Communist Party of India has tried to proclaim its loyalty in its resolution, but it is weighed down by a murky past. We have not forgotten how this party opposed and sabotaged the “Quit India” struggle of 1943, and dubbed Mahatma Gandhi and Netaji Subhas Chandra as traitors and quislings. If it is anxious to prove its bonafides today, it should have a complete break with international Communism, and change its flag, giving it a Bharatiya character and Complexion. It is said ‘that one faction in the party is pro-Chinese, the other Russian. What a pitiful handful is pro-Indian? I have another interesting suggestion: as the party is an expert in the matter of demonstrations and knows well the technique, it should stage a vigorous demonstration, in front of the Chinese Embassy in New Delhi, condemning Chinese aggression and raising slogans against China. That they have not done so up-till now is rather suspicious.

 

Kashmir Problem: Our Government has initiated talks with Pakistan for a settlement of the Kashmir issue and other outstanding problems between the two countries. All men of good will in India and Pakistan will sincerely hope that this latest endeavour will be crowned with success and lead to a lasting amicable settlement. I however believe that no settlement will be of much value or purpose unless and until Pakistan is at the same time made to realise that Communist China is a menace to both India and Pakistan, and the enemy of Asian and World peace.

 

We are deeply grateful to the United States, Britain, France, Canada and other friendly countries for the aid they have rushed to us so promptly and generously. We must steadily build up our military strength, and prepare the country materially and psychologically for the counter-offensive in the near future. The Himalayas are today not a mere physical or geographical barrier. They are an ideological frontier between democracy and communism.

 

The heart of the nation is sound. Only a strong, determined, courageous and dedicated leadership is needed. I hope that Jawaharlal Nehru, who worked and fought for the country in the company of Mahatmaji and Netaji, will rise to the occasion and give such leadership to the Indian people, the men, women, and children of this vast land. If that is forthcoming, then, under God, with hope and faith, the nation will march undaunted, and avenge our reverses, wipe out the shame and humiliation of the past few months, and will go on marching not only to victory in war, but also to a victorious peace. I am sure of that, and we will all acclaim that victorious peace. Jai Hind!
