CARRYING THE CROSS

 

By Dr. MAYADHAR MANSINHA

(Rendered from Oriya)

 

Thou, my Lord, art eternally absorbed in the sorrows of creation;

How little indeed have I done to lessen Thy worries!

I have looked upon the miseries of the universe so far,

In distant indifference or patronising sighs.

 

Absorbed in mine own see kings

I have been deaf to the mute appeals of the distressed,

Arguing, “Whoever is there to look after my needs?

“Where is the place of appeal to redress my wrongs?”

 

Till I knew Thee, my Lord, my little complaints

Looked enormous to my cribbed little self;

But now that I have surrendered my Whole unto Thee

I wish I could share Thy burdens too!

I would fain carry Thy Cross in life after life,

If I had only the vision of Thy feet

Travelling before me along the journey!
