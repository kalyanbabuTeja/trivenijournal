ANANDA COOMARASWAMY

 

The tangled threads of life,

Unravelling which we vainly try,

To thee their windings have been most clear;

The myriad voices and the myriad tongues

Manifest to thee their real unity;

Art of the East and methods of the West,

Knowledge of Life, intuition, sympathy

Have all combined to give thy thought

A singular clarity–clarity refining,

Transforming, welding, into one purposeful whole

What seems so complex, diverse, manifold.

Far from thy home the motherland calls to thee,

Its cherished son. Return, O return to her

Warm embrace to pass the evening of thy Life

Away from the strife, the noise of restless West;

Return to rest and inspire thy brethren,

O Lanka’s jewel, Bharat’s own.

–S. DURAI RAJA SINGAM

 

A poem sent to Ananda Coomaraswamy on seventieth birthday (22 August 1947)
