TO A LOTUS

 

A. VENKATESWARA RAO

 

Feign would I be a Lotus Sweet

Couched in golden lake’s emarald lap

Were it a sight for man to meet!

Or God to muse and fill a gap!

 

Peeping from water pale in hue

Over a world now strange to view

Neath a curtain of vivid blue

Feign would I be a Lotus true.

 

In silence ope’s her petals white

Well God the Almighty to greet

So, well might I, the Lord for light

Feign would I be a Lotus sweet.

 

Alone she smiles and makes for sure

By sullen heart merry and pure

Through thick or thin by day or night

Feign would I be a Lotus white.
